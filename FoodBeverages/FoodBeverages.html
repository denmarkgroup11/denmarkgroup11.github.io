Food

Smorrebrod

This is a traditional Danish lunch that is made up of a slice of dark bread topped with butter, sliced meat, fish, or cheese.

Gule aerter

In English, this is pea soup that is often served with salted pork, carrots and other available vegetables.

Boller i karry

Served with rice and cucumber salad, boller i karry are pork meatballs cooked in a curry sauce.

Apple Charlotte

This is a favourite Danish dessert made from stewed sweetened apples, bread crumbs, crushed almond-flavoured meringue that are all layered. This treat is then topped with whipped cream and redcurrant jelly and is served cold.

Beverages

Tuborg Beer

The Carlsberg has owned the Tuborg Brewery since the 1970s. But even so, it retained its distinct flavor with a special place in beverage history. Although it’s not the most popular drink in Denmark, it became one of the best-selling items during the Christmas season. During this time, thousands of customers await its annual release of a special Christmas ale.

Akvavit

Akvavit is one of the most popular local liquors in Denmark and is made from grains and potatoes. For hundreds of years, the Danish people have been distilling the alcohol and have been used for various cultural ceremonies and practices, including festivals. It has a distinct flavor with hints of spices and herbs, traditionally at least, caraway or dill.

Mead

Considered one of the world's oldest alcoholic beverages, Mead has also been foundational in Danish culture. It's derived from two main ingredients, namely fermented honey and water, with inclusions with spices, fruits, and other flavors. These flavors are typically added once the Mead is ready to drink. It’s like a cider with its warm feature and a sweet taste.

Brennivin

It’s made mainly from grains and potatoes, giving it features and qualities of a vodka. But what makes it different is that the Danish people have been brewing it even before the term Vodka came to formalization. Brennivin is famous for heavy drinkers or those who want to dose off with the spirits.
